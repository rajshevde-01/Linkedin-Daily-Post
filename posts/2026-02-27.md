Most security teams take 24 hours to detect a breach. Attackers now need less than 30 minutes to move laterally across your network. That gap is widening every quarter.

Here are three threats that kept our team on alert this week.

Number one: authentication bypass vulnerabilities in perimeter devices are being weaponized within hours of public disclosure. We tracked active exploitation of firewall management interfaces where the patch had been out for barely a day. If your management plane is internet-facing, treat this as an emergency.

Number two: ransomware groups have shifted their playbook. Over half of ransomware-linked CVEs this year were discovered through active exploitation, not responsible disclosure. The traditional patch cycle is no longer fast enough.

Number three: cloud infrastructure attacks surged 37 percent year over year. The most common entry point we are seeing is overly permissive service accounts that were provisioned once and never reviewed.

One thing worth doing before you sign off today: run a quick audit of your externally exposed management interfaces and stale service account permissions. Fifteen minutes of review can save your weekend.
