### The Vector
We're dealing with `SolarWinds`, `Ivanti`, and `Workspace One` vulnerabilities. 
Actively exploited. 
This changes everything.

### The Exploit
CISA has flagged these as high-risk. Given the nature of these tools, a successful exploit could grant attackers deep access to network management and monitoring systems. We've seen `CVE` scores indicate a high level of severity, suggesting these vulnerabilities can be leveraged for significant malicious activity. Our team is particularly concerned about the potential for lateral movement within a compromised network.

### The Mitigation
We need to patch now. Implementing a robust `patch management` process is crucial. I recommend a swift review of all systems to identify any `SolarWinds`, `Ivanti`, or `Workspace One` installations and apply the necessary patches immediately. A key detection rule to implement would be monitoring for unusual activity from these tools, particularly focusing on `authentication` attempts and `configuration` changes. Set up a rule to alert on any `login` events from unknown `IPs` to these systems.
