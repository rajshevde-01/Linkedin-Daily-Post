Stop telling people to just 'patch faster'. It's lazy advice.

Most organizations can't keep up with the sheer volume of patches. 
Microsoft just released patches for 84 flaws, including two public zero-days. 

What people think is that patching is a simple process. 
But what actually happens is that it's a complex, time-consuming task that requires significant resources. 

We need to focus on vulnerability management, not just patch management. 
It's about prioritizing the most critical vulnerabilities and developing a strategy to mitigate them. 

I'm calling out the patch fatigue mantra. 
It's not working, and it's time for a new approach.
