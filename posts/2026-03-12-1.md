Patching faster is not the answer. 
What people think is that tossing a patch at a vulnerability solves the problem.
 
But that's a simplistic view. 
Most organizations I've worked with have complex, legacy systems that can't just be patched overnight. 
It takes time, resources, and careful planning to ensure that patches don't break critical functions.

Microsoft's latest Patch Tuesday is a case in point, with 84 flaws patched, including two public zero-days. 
That's a significant undertaking, requiring careful testing and deployment. 
We can't just tell people to "patch faster" when we know that's not a realistic solution.
